Tenant Demographics

Is there more than one type of tenant who would rent rooms here?

🟢Strong2 points

Two or more viable tenant demographics — e.g. young professionals AND students, or healthcare workers AND DSS tenants

🟡Moderate1 point

One clear demographic but no obvious fallback if that demand changes

🔴Weak0 points

No clear tenant demographic identified

"The key is diversity. If one demographic dries up — a university closes, a major employer leaves — you need a fallback. Aim for at least two viable tenant types in your target area."

▼ Which demographics should I look for?
  • Young Professionals — Age 22-35, working locally, commuter routes to city centres
  • Students — University within 1-2 miles, 5,000+ student population
  • Healthcare Workers — NHS trust, hospital or large care network within 2 miles
  • DSS / Housing Benefit — Check Local Housing Allowance rates for the area
  • Construction / Contract Workers — Major infrastructure or regeneration projects nearby

Article 4 Directions

Does your target area require planning permission to convert to an HMO?

🟢No Article 42 points

Area has no Article 4 direction — permitted development rights apply, no planning permission needed for C3 to C4 conversion

🟡Article 4 — High approval rate1 point

Article 4 applies but council has a track record of approving HMO applications in the area

🔴Article 4 — Restrictive0 points

Article 4 applies and council is known to refuse or heavily restrict HMO planning applications

"Check your local council's planning portal. Search 'Article 4 HMO [your area]'. If Article 4 applies, look at recent planning decisions to gauge approval rates before committing."

▼ How to check Article 4 in any area
  • Step 1 — Google "[council name] Article 4 HMO direction"
  • Step 2 — Check your local council's planning policy documents
  • Step 3 — Search the planning portal for recent HMO applications nearby
  • Step 4 — Look at the decision notices — approved or refused?
  • Step 5 — Call the planning department — they're usually helpful with pre-application queries

Licensing Requirements

What licensing applies — and is it manageable?

🟢Mandatory licence only2 points

Only mandatory HMO licensing applies (5+ occupants, 3+ storeys). Straightforward requirements, clear process

🟡Additional licensing — manageable1 point

Additional or selective licensing applies but requirements are clear and achievable

🔴Complex or restrictive licensing0 points

Heavy additional licensing requirements, very high fees, or conditions that significantly impact profitability

"Search '[council name] HMO licensing' to find what applies. All HMOs with 5+ tenants need mandatory licensing nationally. Many councils add additional or selective licensing — check fees and conditions carefully."

▼ Types of HMO licensing explained
  • Mandatory Licensing — Applies nationally. 5+ tenants, 3+ storeys. Non-negotiable.
  • Additional Licensing — Council-specific. Can extend to smaller HMOs (e.g. 3+ tenants). Check your council.
  • Selective Licensing — Applies to ALL rental properties in designated areas, not just HMOs.
  • Fees — Typically £500-£1,500 per licence. Factor into your yield calculations.
  • Conditions — Room sizes, fire safety, communal space requirements. Check before you buy.
